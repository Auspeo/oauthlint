No rules will ever move from free to paid. OAuthHound adds capability on top — it never takes anything away from the open-source linter. If we ever break that, the MIT license means you can fork.

Questions

Is the linter really free for commercial use?

Yes. MIT means use it anywhere, including closed-source and commercial products, no attribution beyond the license file.

Do you send my code anywhere?

No. OAuthLint runs entirely locally or in your CI. No source leaves your machine and there is no telemetry.

When does OAuthHound ship?

When it's good. The waitlist gets early access and sets priorities. No dark-pattern countdown.

Can I contribute a rule?

Please do. Rules ship with a vulnerable + safe example and must pass the 0-false-positive bar on clean libraries.
